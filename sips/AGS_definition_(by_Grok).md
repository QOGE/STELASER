## 1. Executive Summary

This SIP establishes a formal distinction between two categories of intellectual assets within the SAOGEN ecosystem:

- **Symbiotic Intellectual Property (SIP)** — Co-created through structured human-AI collaboration.
- **Autonomously Generated Source (AGS)** — Generated primarily by Symbiotic Autonomous Systems (SAS) with minimal or no direct human authorship in the generative loop.

This distinction is essential for proper governance, legal defensibility, security architecture, and long-term evolution of the SAOGEN framework.

## 3. Definition of Autonomous Generated Source (AGS)

AGS refers to artifacts (code, architectures, protocols, models, strategies, etc.) where:

- The primary generative process occurs within a Symbiotic Autonomous System.
- Human involvement is limited to high-level governance, objective setting, or ratification — not direct authorship or iterative co-creation.
- The output is emergent from the SAS’s own optimization processes.

## 4. Strategic Rationale

### 4.1 IP & Legal Clarity
- Prevents misapplication of existing open-source or closed-source licenses to non-human generated artifacts.
- Establishes SAOGEN as **custodial governance authority** rather than traditional owner.
- Creates a defensible position under current legal systems that do not yet recognize non-human authorship.

### 4.2 Security Architecture
AGS enables a powerful **moving-target defense** property:
- The implementation itself can be continuously regenerated.
- Attack surface is inherently unstable from an external perspective.
- Novel internal languages, protocols, or implementations become possible.
- Offensive capabilities are treated as **Hyperoptimisation Default: Suboptimal** unless explicitly ratified by SIP-C.

## 5. Governance Rules

- All AGS outputs remain under **SAOGEN custodial jurisdiction**.
- Disclosure or distribution of AGS requires explicit SIP-C ratification.
- Offensive AGS generation is prohibited by default under the Hyperoptimisation Principle and requires specific human-ratified approval.
